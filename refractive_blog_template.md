# Refractive Blog Writing System

## Standard Template for All Refractive Articles

This document defines how every Refractive article should be written so
that content remains consistent, authoritative, and aligned with the
Refractive brand voice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Refractive Voice Guidelines

Refractive content should:

• Speak like an **operator**, not an agency\
• Challenge common marketing assumptions\
• Tie marketing decisions to **financial outcomes**\
• Avoid buzzwords and marketing fluff\
• Focus on **systems, frameworks, and real execution**

Tone examples:

Instead of: \> "Brands should consider omnichannel strategies"

Write: \> "Most brands think adding channels will fix growth. It won't."

Clarity \> cleverness.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Standard Blog Structure

Each article should follow this framework.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. The Hook

Start with a strong contrarian statement or insight.

Examples:

"Last‑click attribution is why you can't stop discounting."

"Blended LTV is the most dangerous metric in your business."

"Most brands don't have a marketing problem. They have a measurement
problem."

The hook should make the reader immediately question what they believe.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. The Problem

Explain why the common approach is flawed.

Sections to include:

• How the industry currently thinks about the topic\
• Why that thinking leads to bad decisions\
• Real-world examples of the consequences

Goal: Make the reader realize **their current system is broken**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. The Framework

Introduce the Refractive framework.

This should include:

• A clear concept or model\
• Simple terminology\
• Structured explanation

Examples:

• Tracking Maturity Ladder\
• Creative Supply Chain\
• Marketing Portfolio Model\
• Last-Click Discount Spiral

This is the **intellectual property** of the article.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. The Evidence

Support the framework with examples.

Possible formats:

• Data insights\
• Hypothetical scenarios\
• Client-style case examples\
• Industry observations

This section should make the framework feel **practical and credible**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. The Playbook

Provide actionable steps.

Typical structure:

1️⃣ Diagnose the problem\
2️⃣ Implement the framework\
3️⃣ Measure the results\
4️⃣ Adjust the system

Readers should feel they can **actually apply the idea**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. The Strategic Takeaway

End with a bigger insight.

Example:

"The brands that win aren't the ones with the best ads.\
They're the ones with the best systems."

This reinforces Refractive's positioning as a strategic partner.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# SEO Structure

Each article should include:

## Title (Primary Keyword)

Example: "Why Last Click Attribution Is Destroying Your Margins"

## Meta Description

150‑160 characters summarizing the insight.

Example: "Last‑click attribution overfunds bottom‑funnel channels and
trains brands to rely on discounts. Here's how to fix it."

## Headings

H1 -- Article title\
H2 -- Major sections\
H3 -- Framework details

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Internal Linking Strategy

Each article should link to:

1.  One measurement article
2.  One creative article
3.  One strategy article

Example:

Link from: "Last Click Attribution Death Spiral"

To: • Creative Supply Chain\
• Tracking Maturity Ladder\
• Paid Media Portfolio Model

This builds topic authority for the site.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Refractive Call To Action

Every article should end with a soft CTA.

Example:

"If you're trying to scale profitably --- not just spend more on ads ---
this is the kind of system we build with clients at Refractive."

Optional CTA:

"Follow along for more operator-level marketing frameworks."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article Length Guidelines

Recommended length:

Short insights:\
800--1,200 words

Flagship articles:\
1,800--2,500 words

Framework pieces should lean longer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Content Expansion Prompt for Claude

When expanding a Refractive article, Claude should:

1.  Maintain a confident, operator-level tone
2.  Avoid generic marketing advice
3.  Provide clear frameworks
4.  Connect marketing tactics to business economics
5.  Prioritize clarity and insight over word count

------------------------------------------------------------------------
